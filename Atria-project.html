Atria Cooperative

a volunteer non-profit rebrand

Overview

A brand redesign for a non-profit organization that helps poverty in Vancouver, Atria Cooperative. Their goal was to create a more meaningful brand to connect with more people and organize information to make it easier to access for their newer audience. This was a volunteer role.

Roles

UX/UI Design - Visual Design - Wireframing

Team

Lauryn Yau - Firm Kongthong - Tiffany Chan - Areeba Ali

Scope

May 2023 - October 2023 (5 months)

Site and Brand Evaluation

To begin the process of remaking this brand, we had to determine what was wrong with the previous site and branding. We used Nielsen's 10 Heuristics and went through each page of the site to figure out what was and wasn’t working, and we took note of everything.

We found a lot of inconsistencies within their current design, sections that were hard to navigate, and pages with too much unnecessary text, which could cause the user to miss the important information. The site did a poor job of communicating the main message and goal of the organization.

Art Direction and Branding

After the heuristic evaluation, we had to start with temporary branding before the designated branding team came up with a finalized one. To do so, we brainstormed brand keywords, colors, fonts, design direction, as well as interaction types we wanted to implement.

In the end, the branding team also liked this proposal, so we kept it as the final version for the project.

Wireframing

To start our wireframing process, we looked at other websites and took components we liked from them to implement into our own site. For example, we liked the look of bold text on simple backgrounds, asymmetrical grids, and gallery-type image layouts.

We would all design different layouts for each page to start, and then pick elements we liked and combine them into the final wireframe. With this process, we ended up with both the mobile and webpage mockups.

Reflections

Although this project was never sent through to development, I still learned a lot from it. I learned how to do design sprints with my team to come up with a bunch of ideas, and then narrow down from there. I also learned how to do a heuristic evaluation based on Nielsen's 10 principles, which I had never done before. I also now understand how to implement these principles into the entire process of my own design.
