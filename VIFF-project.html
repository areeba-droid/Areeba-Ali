VIFF Microsite

directors at the film festival

Overview

This project was done for the course IAT 235: Information Design split into the two final projects. We were given 8 weeks to pick an existing website and create a related microsite for a specific user group.

Roles

UX Research - UI Design - Visual Design - User Testing

Team

Karina Shuen - Lauryn Yau - Arriyan Ali - Vera Ni - Areeba Ali

Scope

June 2022 - August 2022 (8 Weeks)

User Research

For the first half of this project, my team and I did research on different existing sites that we thought we could add more to. We decided on the Vancouver International Film Festival (VIFF) website, and we found that their core values and goals were to create a diverse and inclusive festival for different film genres.

One thing we found lacking in their website and festival was the opportunity for the incoming audience to network and connect with the existing directors. To fix this, we decided to make a microsite that helps our user group connect and learn from directors at VIFF. To narrow down our users, we decided to focus on film students who had recently or will soon be graduating.

Art Direction

For our design direction, we took inspiration from elements seen in cinema to make it feel more connected to the audience.

Wireframing and Further Research

For the second half of the project, we worked on wireframing and prototyping our microsite for both desktop and mobile. To implement our art direction into our mockups, we added a lot of cascading elements, text overlays, as well as blurred background videos.

A problem we faced while creating the wireframes was that the mobile and desktop looked really different. We wanted the mobile and desktop sites to both have the same feel and use the same elements while still being different, so I brainstormed a couple of ways to do so. In the end, we went with the repetition of cascading elements and horizontal scrolling for both. I also simplified the mobile homepage to have less text than the desktop site due to screen size and readability constraints.

We prototyped our site on Webflow and ended up with an A+ as our overall grade for the project.

Reflections

This project was the first time that I created a user persona to represent my user group and help guide my design, so I think I could have done a better job of creating the persona. I learned a lot about the differences between mobile versus desktop, and how to determine what information is essential for a smaller screen since not everything always fits.
